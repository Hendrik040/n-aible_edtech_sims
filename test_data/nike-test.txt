  Scene 3: Brand Sentiment Deep Dive

  Student goal: Analyze 6 months of Nike social media mentions and product
   reviews. Identify the top 3 brand sentiment drivers, quantify
  share-of-voice by product category, and flag the one emerging negative
  signal the CMO needs to act on.

  Datasets (3 CSVs):

  File: nike_social_mentions.csv
  Rows: ~2,400
  Key columns: platform, date, product_category, sentiment_score,
    sentiment_label, reach, campaign_tag
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  File: nike_product_reviews.csv
  Rows: ~1,800
  Key columns: product_name, category, rating, review_text, source,
    contains_return_mention
  ────────────────────────────────────────
  File: nike_brand_tracker_monthly.csv
  Rows: ~72
  Key columns: month, metric (NPS/awareness/purchase_intent), value,
    segment

  Synthetic patterns baked in (what students must discover):
  - Running and Jordan Brand skew positive (~0.35 mean sentiment)
  - Sustainability has a bimodal distribution — genuine praise +
  greenwashing backlash
  - Training has a sharp negative spike in April 2025 on Reddit (viral
  product quality complaint) — this is the "CMO flag"
  - JustDoIt_Refresh campaign mentions have 5-10x organic reach but mixed
  sentiment — not as positive as the reach implies

  Tasks:
  1. Sentiment distribution by product category (raw + reach-weighted —
  students see rankings change)
  2. Monthly trend chart with anomaly annotation (find the April Training
  drop)
  3. Review return-rate × rating correlation (cross-validates the social
  signal)
  4. Campaign effectiveness snapshot (reach vs sentiment — the
  uncomfortable finding)
  5. Synthesize into a CMO briefing dict with the top 3 headlines

  Starter code scaffold: ~50 lines with # TODO: blocks for each task,
  datasets already loaded, output dict pre-structured.

  File: nike_competitor_review_sentiment.csv
  Rows: ~800
  Key columns: brand, product_name, category, rating, sentiment_score,

  - Nike holds ~54% SOV in Basketball, drops to ~38% in Running (weakest

  3. Demographic index correlation — students must work with the 18-24
  index to surface the On Running threat
  4. Review quality benchmarking table (Nike vs competitors on rating,
  sentiment, review depth)
  5. 2×2 threat matrix (SOV growth × demographic index) → which brand is
  in the "High Threat" quadrant
